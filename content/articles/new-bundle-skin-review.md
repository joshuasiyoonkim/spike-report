Skin reviews should answer one question: does it make you play worse? Everything else is taste. A bundle can look incredible in the shop preview and then quietly cost you duels because the muzzle flash eats your screen. So that's the lens I use.

## The good

- **Inspect animation:** genuinely excellent. One of the cleaner ones in a while.
- **Audio:** crisp, punchy, distinct per-bullet feedback. You can hear your spray pattern, which matters more than people admit.
- **Color story:** the teal-and-chrome variant is gorgeous and, crucially, doesn't wash out on darker maps.

## The not-so-good

The reload is *slow to read*. On a fast re-peek you can lose track of whether you're actually loaded. That's a real gameplay cost, not just a nitpick.

The finisher is a screen-filler. Fun once. By the tenth time it's just a half-second where you can't see the next angle.

## Value verdict

| Tier | Worth it? |
| --- | --- |
| Base skins | Yes, if you like the line |
| Upgrade levels | Only for the audio + VFX you'll actually notice |
| Finisher / card | Skip unless you're a collector |

## Bottom line

Buy the gun, ignore the finisher, and turn the variant to the one that doesn't blind you. A solid bundle that earns its price *if* you stop at the levels that affect feel.
