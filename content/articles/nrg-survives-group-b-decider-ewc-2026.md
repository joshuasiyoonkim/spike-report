NRG is through to playoffs. It just took the scenic route to get there.

## The Group Nobody Wanted

I called Group B the cruelest draw of the season before EWC even started, and it delivered exactly like that. PRX lost to NRG in the opener, then got eliminated by Karmine Corp three days later without a single series win. Vitality beat NRG in the winners match right after. That sent the defending world champions to the loser's bracket, staring down the same team that had just ended PRX's run.

## What Actually Happened

Breeze went to NRG, 13-6. Karmine Corp answered on Fracture, opening with a 9-0 run and closing it out 13-7. Decider map, Haven, and NRG shut the door 13-7. keiko led the box score at 249 ACS on a 55/33/6 line, the kind of stat sheet that reads like someone who decided the season doesn't end on a Tuesday in Paris.

## Why This Actually Matters

Watching that Fracture score, I thought I was about to write the same article twice in one week. Another top seed going home to a hot French team riding a home crowd. Instead NRG did what a defending champion is supposed to do when the alternative is flying home with nothing. They won the map that mattered and moved on.

Vitality and NRG advance from Group B. PRX and Karmine Corp do not. Say what you want about NRG's rough stretch, the Masters London exit, the winners match loss here. They're still standing in the group that ate everyone else alive, and on a day like today, standing is the whole story.
