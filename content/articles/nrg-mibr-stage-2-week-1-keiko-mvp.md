keiko's job this year was never going to be easy. He's replacing s0m, the guy who ran NRG's smokes through their Champions run in Paris, and stepping into a roster that just won a world title. Stage 2 opener against MIBR was his first real answer to "can this guy actually do it," and NRG made him earn it the hard way: down 2-10 on Breeze with the series already 0-1.

## The Breeze Save Is the Whole Story

MIBR took Sunset 13-9, no real drama there. Breeze looked like it was heading the same direction fast, NRG down 2-10 and looking like a team about to open 0-2. Then they just didn't lose another one that mattered. Round after round back, they clawed it to 13-11 and forced a decider. That's not a stat line, that's a team refusing to go down in their first series of the stage. Summit was the formality after that, 13-4, and by the time it ended keiko had the MVP: 227 ACS, 152 ADR, 77.8% KAST across the series.

## Is He the Guy?

One series doesn't answer that. s0m was a franchise piece for years, and keiko spent his 2025 season flexing onto smokes for Team Liquid in EMEA, a different assignment than the one he's got now as NRG's new duelist. But if the bar for game one was "don't let the seat feel empty," he cleared it by a mile. NRG were ten rounds from an 0-2 start to their season, and instead they're 1-0 with the new guy holding the trophy.

Stage 2 is long. Ask me about keiko again in September. Today, though, the numbers don't lie.
