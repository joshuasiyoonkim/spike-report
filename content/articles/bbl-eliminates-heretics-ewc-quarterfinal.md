Team Heretics won't be back-to-back EWC champions. BBL Esports took them down 2-1 in today's quarterfinal, and the run that almost ended in the group stage finally did, one round short of the semis.

## Same Warning, Worse Result

Six days ago I wrote that Heretics' head coach Neilzinho has been coaching this entire tournament remotely while recovering from a torn ACL back home, and that the gap showed up against All Gamers before Heretics clawed back to win in three. I said a remote coach can still call good rounds, he just can't be in the room when a team needs someone to reset it live. Today there was no third-map miracle.

## How It Went

Heretics opened by taking Breeze 16-14 in double overtime, the kind of map-one win that should set the tone for a series. It didn't hold. BBL ran them off Lotus 13-2, with Crewen posting a series-high 260 ACS. Sunset was the decider, and BBL closed it out 13-10, controlling the map from the front for most of the half.

That's not a fluke stat line. That's a team that won the coin-flip map and then got outclassed on both of the ones that actually mattered.

## What This Means

BBL moves on to the semifinals. Heretics go home without defending the title they won off a reverse sweep over Fnatic last year. I'm not saying Neilzinho's ACL is the whole story, teams lose to better play all the time. But when I flag a structural problem in the group stage and the team gets eliminated by it in the quarterfinal, that's not a coincidence I'm willing to ignore.
